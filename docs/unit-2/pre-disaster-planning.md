# Pre-Disaster Planning

Pre-disaster planning is the phase where actions are taken before a disaster strikes to ensure readiness.

## Components of Pre-Disaster Planning

### 1. Risk Assessment and Monitoring

* **Hazard Mapping:** Identifying potential hazards in an area.
* **Early Warning Systems (EWS):**
  * *Example:* **IMD's SATARK** system in Odisha regarding cyclones.
  * *Link:* [Indian Tsunami Early Warning Centre (ITEWC)](https://incois.gov.in/ITEWC/)

![Early Warning System](../assets/images/early_warning_system_chain.png)
*Fig 1: Components of an End-to-End Early Warning System.*

### 2. Prevention and Mitigation

* Implementing long-term measures (e.g., NCRMP - National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project).
  * *Link:* [NCRMP Official Site](https://ncrmp.gov.in/)

### 3. Preparedness

Preparedness involves building the capacity to respond effectively.
> **UNDRR Definition:** "The knowledge and capacities developed by governments, response and recovery organizations, communities and individuals to effectively anticipate, respond to, and recover from, the impacts of likely, imminent or current hazard events or conditions."  
> [Source: UNDRR Terminology](https://www.undrr.org/terminology/preparedness)

* **Contingency Planning:** Developing specific plans (e.g., District Disaster Management Plans - DDMPs).
* **Mock Drills:** Conducting mock drills (e.g., annual nationwide drills by NDRF).

### Descriptive Questions

1. **What are the key components of Pre-Disaster Planning?**
    <details>
    <summary>Click to see Key Points</summary>
    *   **Risk Assessment:** Hazard mapping and vulnerability analysis.
    *   **Prevention & Mitigation:** Structural (dams) and non-structural (laws) measures.
    *   **Preparedness:** EWS, contingency planning, resource stockpiling, and training/drills.
    </details>

2. **Why are Early Warning Systems (EWS) critical in disaster management?**
    <details>
    <summary>Click to see Key Points</summary>
    *   They provide lead time to take action (evacuation).
    *   They significantly reduce loss of life (e.g., Cyclone Phailin vs 1999 Super Cyclone).
    *   They protect livelihoods by allowing people to secure assets.
    </details>
