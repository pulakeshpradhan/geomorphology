# During Disaster Management (Response)

This phase, also known as the **Response** phase, occurs immediately after a disaster strikes.

## Key Activities

### 1. Warning and Evacuation

* Disseminating immediate alerts.
* **Case Study:** The successful evacuation of over 1 million people during Cyclone Phailin (2013) in Odisha is a global benchmark.
  * *Article:* [UN Praise for Odisha's Phailin Response](https://news.un.org/en/story/2013/10/453182)

### 2. Search and Rescue (SAR)

* Deployment of specialized forces (NDRF, ODRAF).
* **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Every operation follows a strict SOP defined by the [NDMA Guidelines on Incident Response System (IRS)](https://ndma.gov.in/sites/default/files/PDF/Guidelines/Guidelines-on-Incident-Response-System.pdf).

### 3. First Aid and Medical Care

* Providing immediate assistance.
* *Link:* [Red Cross India - Disaster Response](https://www.indianredcross.org/disaster-management.html)

![Medical Triage System](../assets/images/triage_system_visual.png)
*Fig 1: Triage Protocol for Mass Casualty Incidents.*

### 4. Relief Operations

* Relief Camps, Food, Water, Sanitation.
* **Sphere Standards:** Humanitarian response is often guided by the Sphere Standards.
  * *Link:* [The Sphere Handbook](https://spherestandards.org/handbook/)

## Institutional Response

* **National Crisis Management Committee (NCMC):** Reviews the situation at the national level.
* **State Executive Committee (SEC):** Coordinates at the state level.

### Descriptive Questions

1. **Describe the key activities undertaken during the "Response" phase of a disaster.**
    <details>
    <summary>Click to see Key Points</summary>
    *   **Evacuation:** Moving people to safety.
    *   **SAR:** Rescuing trapped individuals.
    *   **First Aid:** Providing emergency medical care.
    *   **Relief:** Providing food, clean water, and temporary shelter.
    *   **Communication:** Restoring lines to coordinate efforts.
    </details>

2. **What is the Incident Response System (IRS) and why is it important?**
    <details>
    <summary>Click to see Key Points</summary>
    *   **Definition:** A standardized organizational structure for managing emergency response.
    *   **Importance:** It ensures unity of command, clear roles and responsibilities, and effective coordination between different agencies (Police, Fire, Hospitals) to avoid chaos.
    </details>
